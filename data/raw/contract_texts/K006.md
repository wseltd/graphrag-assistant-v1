## Background

Clearwater Infrastructure PLC owns and operates an estate of telecommunications network and data infrastructure assets across the United Kingdom. Following a cyber resilience review commissioned in the first half of 2025, the Client identified material gaps in its existing continuity arrangements, particularly around recovery time objectives for critical control systems, failover sequencing across geographically distributed sites, and documented escalation paths for major incident response.

Following a structured procurement exercise, Clearwater Infrastructure PLC selected Pinnacle Contracts PLC as its preferred partner for a structured Disaster Recovery Planning engagement. The selection was informed by the Consultant's documented programme delivery record, its demonstrated familiarity with UK critical infrastructure continuity frameworks, and its capacity to complete a full planning engagement within the Client's required timescale.

## Scope of Services

Pinnacle Contracts PLC shall deliver the Disaster Recovery Planning engagement to Clearwater Infrastructure PLC under the terms set out in this Agreement. The engagement is structured in two sequential phases.

Phase One comprises a risk and impact assessment covering the Client's twelve highest-criticality systems, identification of single points of failure, documentation of upstream and downstream recovery dependencies, and the production of a written Business Impact Analysis report. Phase One shall be completed and submitted to the Client within six weeks of the Effective Date.

Phase Two comprises development of the full Disaster Recovery Plan, including system recovery runbooks, defined recovery time and recovery point objectives for each in-scope system, inter-system dependency mappings, and a tested contact escalation directory. The Consultant shall facilitate a tabletop exercise with the Client's operational team to validate the documented recovery procedures prior to final delivery. Phase Two shall be completed and submitted to the Client within fourteen weeks of the Effective Date.

The Client's nominated contract manager for this engagement is William Clearwater, Finance Director of Clearwater Infrastructure PLC. William Clearwater holds authority to approve phase deliverables, authorise any changes to the agreed scope, and formally certify completion of the engagement on behalf of the Client.

The Disaster Recovery Planning engagement shall be charged at the fixed fee of twenty-eight thousand pounds sterling (£28,000.00) exclusive of VAT, payable in two equal instalments.

The first instalment of fourteen thousand pounds sterling (£14,000.00) is due within thirty (30) calendar days of the Effective Date. The second instalment of fourteen thousand pounds sterling (£14,000.00) is due within thirty (30) calendar days of the Client's written acceptance of the Phase Two deliverables.

All invoices shall reference contract number K006 and be addressed to the accounts payable function of Clearwater Infrastructure PLC. Payment shall be made by BACS transfer to the bank account notified by Pinnacle Contracts PLC in writing. Any invoice not formally disputed within ten (10) business days of receipt shall be deemed accepted. Overdue amounts shall accrue interest at three per cent (3%) per annum above the Bank of England base rate, compounding monthly.

## Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement by giving thirty (30) calendar days' written notice to the other at any time before the commencement of Phase Two. Once Phase Two has commenced, termination shall not affect the Client's obligation to pay fees for work already completed and delivered. Either Party may terminate immediately on written notice if the other commits a material breach and, where capable of remedy, fails to remedy it within twenty-one (21) calendar days of written notice. Clearwater Infrastructure PLC may terminate with immediate effect if Pinnacle Contracts PLC enters insolvency proceedings or ceases to carry on business.

Upon termination, the Consultant shall deliver all completed and work-in-progress deliverables to the Client within ten (10) business days. Any fees paid in advance for services not yet delivered shall be refunded to Clearwater Infrastructure PLC within thirty (30) calendar days of the termination date.
