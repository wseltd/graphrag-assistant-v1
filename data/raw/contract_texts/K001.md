# Software Licence Agreement — Contract K001

**Effective Date:** 1 April 2025

**Parties:**

This Software Licence Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of 1 April 2025 by and between:

**Meridian Holdings Ltd**, a company incorporated in England and Wales with registration number 12847391, whose registered office is at 45 King Street, London, EC2V 8RF (hereinafter "the Buyer"); and

**Nexus Procurement PLC**, a company incorporated in England and Wales with registration number 09234156, whose registered office is at 12 Victoria Road, Manchester, M1 2WN (hereinafter "the Supplier").

Together the Buyer and the Supplier are referred to as "the Parties."

---

## Background

The Buyer requires a comprehensive enterprise resource planning system to consolidate its financial management, purchase order processing, and operations reporting functions across its UK business units. Following a competitive tender process, the Buyer has selected the Supplier on the basis of functionality, total cost of ownership, and implementation track record.

The Supplier has agreed to provide the Enterprise Resource Planning Suite, a fully integrated software platform encompassing accounts payable, purchase order management, inventory control, and financial reporting modules. This Agreement sets out the terms under which the Supplier will license and implement that system.

## Scope of Supply

The Supplier shall deliver one (1) perpetual, non-exclusive, non-transferable licence for the Enterprise Resource Planning Suite. The licence entitles Meridian Holdings Ltd to deploy the software across all UK-registered business units and subsidiaries for the duration of the licence term. The system shall be installed on server infrastructure owned and operated by the Buyer at its London premises.

Implementation services shall be delivered within sixteen (16) weeks of the Effective Date. The Supplier shall allocate up to twenty (20) consultant days of on-site support, including project management, configuration, data migration, and end-user training sessions. The Supplier shall provide Meridian Holdings Ltd with monthly progress reports throughout the implementation phase.

The Buyer's nominated contract manager for this engagement is James Blackwood, Chief Procurement Officer of Meridian Holdings Ltd. James Blackwood holds authority to approve delivery milestones, authorise scope changes in writing, and formally accept the delivered system on behalf of the Buyer. The Supplier's nominated contract lead is Sarah Chen, Head of Contracts at Nexus Procurement PLC. Sarah Chen is responsible for contract compliance, milestone certification, invoice submission, and escalation management throughout the term.

## Payment Terms

The total licence fee for the Enterprise Resource Planning Suite is eighty-five thousand pounds sterling (£85,000.00), exclusive of VAT applicable at the prevailing rate. The fee is structured in three tranches as follows:

- **First tranche:** thirty per cent (£25,500.00), due within fourteen (14) calendar days of the execution date of this Agreement.
- **Second tranche:** forty per cent (£34,000.00), due within fourteen (14) calendar days of the Buyer's written sign-off on the User Acceptance Testing phase.
- **Third tranche:** thirty per cent (£25,500.00), due sixty (60) calendar days after the confirmed go-live date, subject to no material defects remaining unresolved.

All invoices shall be addressed to the accounts payable function of Meridian Holdings Ltd and must reference the contract number K001 on their face. Payments shall be made by BACS transfer to the bank account notified by Nexus Procurement PLC in writing. Any invoice not disputed within ten (10) business days of receipt shall be deemed accepted. Overdue amounts shall accrue interest at four per cent (4%) per annum above the Bank of England base rate, compounding monthly. The Supplier reserves the right to suspend ongoing support services if any undisputed invoice remains unpaid for more than thirty (30) calendar days after its due date.

## Confidentiality

Each Party acknowledges that in the course of performing its obligations under this Agreement it may receive or access Confidential Information belonging to the other. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, pricing schedules, system architecture documentation, business process specifications, source code, financial data, and personal data processed through the Enterprise Resource Planning Suite.

Neither Party shall disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the disclosing Party, except where disclosure is required by law or by order of a competent court or regulatory authority. Each Party shall apply no less than the same standard of care to protect the other's Confidential Information as it applies to its own confidential material, and in no event less than reasonable professional care. This obligation shall survive termination or expiry of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years.

## Liability

The Supplier's total aggregate liability to the Buyer under or in connection with this Agreement, whether arising in contract, tort (including negligence), breach of statutory duty, or otherwise, shall not exceed one hundred per cent (100%) of the total fees paid by Meridian Holdings Ltd to Nexus Procurement PLC under this Agreement in the twelve (12) months immediately preceding the event giving rise to the claim.

Neither Party shall be liable to the other for indirect or consequential loss, loss of profit, loss of business opportunity, loss of anticipated revenue, or loss of goodwill, even if that Party had been advised of the possibility of such losses. Nothing in this Agreement excludes or limits either Party's liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, for fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation, or for any liability that cannot lawfully be limited or excluded.

## Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement by giving ninety (90) calendar days' written notice to the other Party at any time after the first anniversary of the Effective Date. Either Party may also terminate immediately on written notice if the other commits a material breach of any term of this Agreement and, where the breach is capable of remedy, fails to remedy it within thirty (30) calendar days of receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.

Meridian Holdings Ltd may terminate with immediate effect if Nexus Procurement PLC becomes insolvent, enters administration, is subject to a winding-up petition, or makes any composition or arrangement with its creditors generally. Upon termination for any reason, the Buyer shall promptly cease using the licensed software and shall, at the Supplier's election, return or securely destroy all copies of the Enterprise Resource Planning Suite and associated documentation, providing written certification of destruction within ten (10) business days. Any prepaid licence fees attributable to periods falling after the effective date of termination shall be refunded to Meridian Holdings Ltd on a pro-rata basis within thirty (30) calendar days.
